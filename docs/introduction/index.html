Architectural design approach

This architecture description (AD) is informed by the principles outlined in DS/ISO/IEC/IEEE 42010:2022 (ISO 42010:2022)(TODO ref) (in particular clause 6), the international standard for architecture descriptions of systems and software. While this AD leverages ISO 42010 as a guiding framework, the authors make no claims that it is compliant with all aspects of the standard.

The viewpoints used in this AD are drawn from the comprehensive set defined by Rozanski and Woods [2], which is based on the 2011 version of ISO 42010(TODO ref). The viewpoints used in this AD include:

  • Context Viewpoint: Describes the system's external interfaces, dependencies, and interactions with its environment to provide a high-level understanding of its operational context.
  • Functional Viewpoint: Focuses on the system's functional structure, detailing the responsibilities of major components and their interactions to fulfill required capabilities.
  • Information Viewpoint: Examines the system's key data structures, their relationships, and the flow of information within and between components.
  • Concurrency Viewpoint: Addresses the system's runtime behavior, including threading, processes, and mechanisms for coordination and synchronization.
  • Development Viewpoint: Highlights the system's construction, including module organization, development tools, and build processes, to support the needs of developers.
  • Deployment Viewpoint: Details the mapping of software artifacts to hardware infrastructure, capturing the operational environment and deployment strategies.
  • Operational Viewpoint: Focuses on the system's ongoing management, monitoring, and operational processes to ensure stability and performance.

No claims are made that the specifications of these viewpoints found in [2] are compliant with either version of ISO 42010.

Many of the architecture view components used in this AD, as well as their specifications, are adapted from [2] and [1]. Note that the former, being based on ISO 42010:2011, uses terminology incompatible with ISO 42010:2022. Note also that the latter is not based on either version of ISO 42010, and its terminology also conflicts with that of the standard.
